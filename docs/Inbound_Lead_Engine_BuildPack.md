## 2. The offer — "Free Land Feasibility Snapshot"

The hook that beats every wholesaler: you don't ask to buy their land cheap — you tell them **what it could become**.

**What the owner gets (you produce it in ~30 min using Land ID):**
- Flood zone & wetlands check
- Easements & access review
- Topography / buildable area
- Utility proximity (water, sewer, power)
- Zoning & likely use
- Rough lot-yield / highest-best-use estimate
- One-page PDF + a 5-minute Loom walkthrough

**Two variants of the same magnet:**
- **Owners:** "Find out what your land is really worth — and what it could become." -> feeds Acquisition / JV.
- **Builders/investors:** "Get a buildability snapshot before you buy / get first look at shovel-ready lots." -> feeds Disposition / Consulting.

Why it converts: it's genuinely valuable, it's fast, it shows your expertise, and it naturally leads to "want me to help you develop / sell / partner on this?"

**The offer ladder (free-call-first).** Lead with the free feasibility call as the only entry. Keep these priced tiers documented and switch them on once demand is proven — for now every CTA points to the free call and the AI never quotes prices:
- **Free feasibility & value call** — the CTA on everything (start here)
- $750 — Basic Land Screen
- $2,500 — Full Due Diligence
- $5,000 — Builder-Ready Package
- JV / equity partnership to build

## 2b. Sharper targeting + two new inbound sources

**Sharper targeting niche (who your content + research should focus on):**
Stop thinking "any owner." Focus the content, the feasibility teardowns, and any research on developer-grade targets:
- Unplatted / "TBD" (no street address) parcels
- Ag-exempt land near growth corridors
- Infill teardown candidates (older structure on 0.5+ acre)
- Surplus municipal / government land

**Data layer — add Regrid (alongside Land ID).** Regrid does a different job than Land ID: it's the bulk parcel-data + API layer (owner info, polygons, handles TBD parcels) for research and list-building. Land ID stays your visual deep-dive for the actual feasibility snapshot.

**Two new inbound sources (relationship-driven — pure inbound):**
1. **Referral partner network** — civil engineers, land-use attorneys, surveyors, and title companies. Add 10-15 to GHL, tag `partner`, send a warm intro + your sample snapshot.
2. **Private-capital network** — reframe your HNW IT/doctor contacts as *capital partners* for cash acquisitions. Funds deals without a raise.

### 3c. First 10 posts (ready to publish)

**Post 1 — Teardown**
This 12-acre parcel looked perfect. Then I pulled it up in Land ID. Half of it sits in the floodway. The "road frontage" is a recorded easement, not legal access. The seller priced it like 12 buildable acres. It's really about 5. This is why a feasibility check before you buy isn't optional — it's the difference between a deal and a lawsuit. Own a parcel you're unsure about? I'll run you a free snapshot. Comment "LAND" or DM me.

**Post 2 — Market**
DFW added more people last year than most states. Every one of them needs a roof. Builders aren't short on demand — they're short on shovel-ready lots. Whoever controls developable land controls the pipeline. If you own raw land near a growth corridor, you're holding the scarcest thing in this market.

**Post 3 — Educational**
"Developable" doesn't mean "flat and empty." Five things that actually decide it: 1) Legal access (not just a dirt road), 2) Utilities within reach, 3) Floodplain & wetlands, 4) Easements & encumbrances, 5) Zoning vs. what the city will actually approve. Miss one and your "10 lots" becomes 4.

**Post 4 — Story/credibility**
The most expensive lesson I learned developing 400+ acres: the cheapest land is usually cheap for a reason. Early on I chased a low price per acre and inherited a drainage problem that ate the margin. Now I'd rather pay more for clean dirt with access and utilities. Price per acre is the headline. Feasibility is the story.

**Post 5 — Offer/CTA**
I'll make you a deal. Send me a Texas parcel you own (or are thinking of buying) and I'll run a free feasibility snapshot — flood zone, easements, access, rough lot yield. One page, no pitch. If it turns into something we can work on together, great. If not, you keep the analysis. Drop the address below or DM me.

**Post 6 — Teardown**
Two parcels. Same size. Same county. One is worth 3x the other. The difference? One has sewer at the property line. The other needs a $400k extension. Buyers see acres. Developers see infrastructure.

**Post 7 — Investor angle**
A lot of the doctors and IT folks I talk to want real estate exposure but don't want to be landlords. Land development is the version nobody tells them about: you're not fixing toilets, you're creating lots builders compete for. The catch — it's all in the underwriting and entitlement. That's the part I run.

**Post 8 — JV explainer**
"I own land but I don't have capital to develop it." You don't need capital. You need a partner who does the work. A land-owner JV: you contribute the dirt, I run feasibility -> entitlement -> shovel-ready, we split the upside. No money out of your pocket.

**Post 9 — Market/educational**
Raw land -> shovel-ready lots is a value jump most owners never capture. They sell raw, the developer captures the entitlement spread. Sometimes selling raw is right. Sometimes a JV doubles what you walk away with. The only way to know is to run the numbers on YOUR parcel.

**Post 10 — Offer/CTA**
Quick reminder of what I actually do: tell you if your land is developable (free snapshot); manage the whole thing if it is; partner with you instead of buying you out (JV). No cold offers, no pressure. Own land in Texas? Send me the address.

## 4. Capture — the Interactive Feasibility Quiz (centerpiece)

A self-serve "Is my land buildable / what's it worth?" quiz (~90 sec) that delivers instant value AND scores the lead silently. Build it GHL-native first; a custom Regrid-powered web app is a v2 upgrade.

### 4a. The 7 steps
1) Property address or county? 2) Owner / broker / builder-developer? 3) Sell / subdivide / build / just find value? 4) How soon? 5) Raw / partially improved / shovel-ready? 6) Acreage? 7) Name + email + phone (+ SMS opt-in).

### 4b. Instant value on submit
Auto-send a short Feasibility Snapshot teaser: likely zoning considerations, "what owners in {county} typically build," the 3 paths (sell as-is / subdivide / JV-to-build), CTA to book the free call. Polished one-page Land ID snapshot follows from Mir within 2-3 business days.

### 4c. Where it lives
Embedded on the site landing page; standalone link for LinkedIn bio/posts/ads and referral partners; GHL funnel mirror.

### 4d. On submit (auto)
Create/update contact -> write fields -> tag inbound, feasibility-quiz, source, persona, goal -> create opportunity in Land Development Consulting / New Lead -> fire scoring + matching nurture track.

## 5. Qualify — conversation script + scoring

**Persona prompt:** You are the assistant for New Door Investments, Mir Ali's DFW land development firm. Tone: warm, professional, concise — never pushy. Thank the lead, confirm details, ask up to 3 qualifying questions, book a 15-min call. Never make offers or quote prices.

**Qualifying questions (max 3):** 1) Confirm parcel address/APN and county. 2) Selling, developing, partnering, or exploring? 3) Timeline?

### 5a. Fit x Intent score (0-100)
Fit: owns developable land +20; decision-maker +20; builder/dev seeking lots +20; persona matches ICP +15; capital partner +10. Intent: timeline now/<3mo +25; submitted specific parcel +20; need=build/subdivide +20; completed full quiz +15; replied/asked +15.
**Bands:** 70+ hot (auto-book + alert) · 40-69 warm (nurture) · <40 cold (self-serve).
